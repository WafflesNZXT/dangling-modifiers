Writing with Clarity

Finding and Fixing Modifier Mistakes

Wafi Syed

The Golden Rule of Modifiers

"Covered in hot fudge, my husband ate the sundae."

What we meant

The sundae has the fudge.

What we actually said

The husband is covered in fudge.

A modifier (a descriptive phrase) must be placed right next to the word it describes.

Two Common Mistakes

01. Misplaced Modifiers

The description is in the wrong spot. It's too far from the right word, so it looks like it's describing the wrong thing.

The Fix: Move the phrase closer.

02. Dangling Modifiers

The word being described is missing from the sentence entirely. The description is just "dangling" there with no subject.

The Fix: Rewrite to add the subject.

Class Practice: Finding the Subject

"Screaming and crying, the mother calmed the baby."

What it sounds like

The mother is the one screaming and crying while trying to be helpful.

How to fix it

"The mother calmed the baby who was screaming and crying."

Task: Who is really doing the action?
